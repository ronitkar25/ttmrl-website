Our Mission Statement

Led by Dr. Susan Shea, PhD and Dr. Matthew Neal, MD, FACS, the Trauma and Transfusion Medicine Research Laboratory (TTMRL) is focused on improving clinical outcomes and quality of life for patients with trauma injury through improved prevention and interventions by conducting basic and translational science to using implementation and dissemination science principles to improve adoption of preventative measures to reduce traumatic injury, by describing the pathophysiology of traumatic injury, and advancing surgical and therapeutic strategies.

The mission also includes improving the effectiveness and safety of blood products for all critically ill patients by investigating the differential biologic effects of blood product manufacturing and leveraging these differences to achieve precision transfusion medicine where the blood products transfused is optimal based on the patients' clinical condition and genetic predisposition.

The TTMRL is uniquely positioned as part of the University of Pittsburgh and next to the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center (UPMC). This has led to numerous opportunities for IRB-approved scientific investigation involving human patient samples, allowing us to optimize the clinical relevance of our work.


Our Team

The TTMRL team is an extraordinary group of individuals, each bringing their own expertise, experiences, and perspectives to our work. Together, we create a space where ideas collide, evolve, and lead to exciting new discoveries. It’s our team’s curiosity, creativity, and dedication that drive everything we do. We couldn’t be prouder of the people who make up TTMRL. Their passion for exploration and their commitment to each other create an environment where everyone feels empowered and supported.
